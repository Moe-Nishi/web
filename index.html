Climate of Spain

The central part of the country, such as Madrid and Toledo, has a continental climate with hot summers and cold winters. Barcelona and other parts of Catalonia and Andalusia in the south have a Mediterranean climate that is warm and pleasant. Spring is a time of great temperature fluctuations. Clothing that can be easily taken on and off is convenient. The mornings and evenings can be chilly. Summer is extremely hot in Andalusia. The humidity is relatively low, so it feels cooler in the shade. However, the sun is intense, so sunglasses and hats are a must. Autumn is almost the same as autumn in Japan. However, the temperature varies greatly from one day to the next, so be sure to bring a thick sweater. In the winter, Madrid and other parts of central Spain are often cold and some places even get snow. Don't forget to take adequate protection against the cold.

Measures to avoid being a victim of crime

Do not carry large amounts of money. Be wary of people approaching you. If someone tries to distract you, watch your belongings.
